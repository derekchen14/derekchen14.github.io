## intro
July is where most beginner gardens go to die. It’s not because you’re a bad gardener; it’s because the honeymoon phase of spring is over and the real heat has arrived. If you don't adjust your strategy now, your plants won't make it to August.

## the july slump
By mid-summer, the soil dries out faster than you realize. Your plants are working overtime to stay cool, and if they hit their breaking point, they stop producing fruit entirely. This mid-season "slump" is the biggest hurdle for any new grower.

## watering mistakes
Most people water their gardens in the middle of the day, but that's actually the worst time to do it. The water evaporates before it reaches the roots, and droplets on leaves can act like tiny magnifying glasses for the sun. You should be watering deeply at the base of the plant during the early morning instead.

## heat stress signs
Keep an eye out for yellowing leaves or stems that look limp even after a watering. If your tomatoes are dropping their blossoms or your peppers look scorched, those are clear signals that the heat is winning. Catching these signs early is the only way to save the harvest.

## what to do instead
Add a thick layer of mulch to keep the moisture in the ground and consider using a shade cloth during the hottest part of the afternoon. These small shifts make a massive difference in whether your garden thrives or withers away before fall.
